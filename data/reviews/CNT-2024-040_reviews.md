# Contract Review - CNT-2024-040
## PetroLogistics ME - A Strategic Dilemma

### Review Period
**Q1-Q2 2024** (January - June 2024)  
**Reviewer**: Hamed Al-Jabri, Logistics Supervisor  
**Review Date**: July 21, 2024  
**Contract Status**: CRITICAL REVIEW

---

## Overall Assessment: **UNPARALLELED CAPACITY vs. UNACCEPTABLE RISK**

PetroLogistics (PL) presents a unique challenge for Daleel. Operationally, they are our most reliable mover of heavy rigs and bulk equipment. However, their internal safety culture—as evidenced by repeated IVMS violations—is a "ticking time bomb."

## The Good
1. **Ramping Capacity**: Successfully moved all 8 rigs during the South-Field expansion without a single scheduling delay.
2. **Reliability**: Volume targets (moves per month) are consistently 5% above the industry average.

## The Bad
1. **Safety Conduct**: The February spill was entirely preventable. Subsequent IVMS audits show high-range speeding is still occurring (88% compliance vs 100% target).
2. **Reactive Management**: PL management only seems to fix issues when we threaten to stop the contract.

## Conclusion
We are **HEAVILY DEPENDENT** on PL because no other local vendor has their specialized heavy-lift fleet available for our 2025 projects. We must choose between operational delays (switching vendors) or high safety risk (staying with the status quo).
